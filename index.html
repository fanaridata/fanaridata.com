What our dashboards look like

These are sample portfolio dashboards built on fictional data. They demonstrate the type of executive visibility, forecasting capability, and business intelligence we deliver to clients.

Case Study · Executive Dashboard
Executive KPI Scorecard — Multi-Location Business
Revenue visibility ROAS tracking Operations

Business problem: Leadership at a multi-location retail business had no single view of performance across regions. Each location reported separately, making it impossible to compare performance or identify problems early.

Approach: We consolidated all location data into a unified executive scorecard showing revenue, gross profit, ROAS, average order value, on-time delivery, and customer metrics — updated monthly with trend indicators.

Outcome: Leadership could immediately see which locations were underperforming and where ROAS was weakest, enabling targeted budget reallocation and a 12% lift in overall margin within two quarters.

Executive Dashboard · Analytics
Monthly Revenue & Gross Profit Trend
Trend analysis Margin tracking

Business problem: An owner knew revenue was growing but had no visibility into whether gross profit was keeping pace — or whether rising costs were quietly eroding margin.

Approach: Built a 24-month trend dashboard overlaying revenue and gross profit, with automatic margin % calculation.

Outcome: Identified a 3-month period where revenue grew but gross profit flatlined — tracing it to a supplier price increase that hadn't been passed to customers. The owner renegotiated pricing within weeks.

Executive Dashboard · Regional
Revenue & Gross Profit by Region
Regional comparison Underperformance detection

Business problem: A business with four regional locations was allocating ad spend equally across all regions — despite significant differences in performance.

Approach: Designed a regional comparison dashboard showing revenue, gross profit, and customer count by location side by side.

Outcome: Revealed one region generating 34% less gross profit per customer than the others. Ad spend was reallocated toward higher-performing regions, improving overall ROAS by 18%.

Case Study · Forecasting
Revenue Forecast with Confidence Range
Demand forecasting Planning Confidence intervals

Business problem: Leadership needed a clearer view of revenue trends and a reliable basis for planning — hiring decisions, inventory commitments, and marketing budgets were all being made without any forward-looking model.

Approach: Built a monthly revenue forecasting model using historical actuals, applied statistical trend analysis, and generated a 6-month forward projection with upper and lower confidence bounds. Actual vs. forecast variance is tracked monthly to continuously improve model accuracy.

Insight delivered: Recent revenue trend supports continued growth, but variance monitoring flagged two months where actuals fell below forecast — prompting investigation that identified a seasonal demand shift the team hadn't previously quantified. Forward planning was adjusted accordingly.

Executive Dashboard · Product Analysis
Revenue Mix by Product Line
Product mix analysis Trend by SKU Revenue concentration

Business problem: A business owner knew which products sold the most units, but had no clear picture of how each product line's revenue contribution was trending over time — or whether the business was becoming over-reliant on a single line.

Approach: Built a stacked revenue mix chart showing monthly contribution by product line across a 24-month period, making shifts in mix immediately visible.

Outcome: Revealed that the Premium Service line had grown from 45% to 58% of total revenue over 18 months — a concentration risk the owner hadn't noticed. The dashboard prompted a deliberate strategy to grow the Core Service line as a hedge.
